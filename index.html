The U.S. spent more on gifts this holiday season on average than years past

by Andriana Kourkoumelis | December 22nd, 2024

Americans had record spending this year after Black Friday and Cyber Monday, especially prioritizing holiday gifts for children.

This holiday season, the U.S. hit record numbers in spending across popular retail categories, including top performers like toys and technology. According to data by Adobe Analytics, sales of toys and games across retailers were up 622% when compared to off-season shopping. Apparel and technology were also up by over 300% around Black Friday and Cyber Monday sales. Overall, when compared to 2023 holiday shopping has increased about 3% as of November 2024 based on data collected by Mastercard.

Parents in particular shopped more for their children this year than last year, though 49% expect to go into debt after November and December purchases according to a survey done by Coupon Birds.

Home and gifts editor at CNN Underscored Amina Lake Abdelrahman said that toys and electronics did extremely well after Black Friday and Cyber Monday coverage.

“Our SEO team agrees that parents went all out on their kids this year, we can expect more toys and games coverage next holiday season. As always technology does really well also especially for those kids wanting a new iPad,” Abdelrahman said.

Abdelrahman said that her team thinks that toys are making a comeback with top products being LEGO sets and Wicked toys, especially when they are on sale.
